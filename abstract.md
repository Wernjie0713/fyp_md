# Abstract

This project proposes the development of a company-owned Sales and Payment Analytics Platform to reduce operational risk associated with dependence on a third-party cloud point-of-sale (POS) reporting portal in a large-scale food and beverage (F&B) enterprise. In the current setting, Finance and Operations stakeholders rely on vendor-managed exports and portal interfaces for routine reconciliation and month-end closing, which constrains ad hoc analysis, limits transparency into underlying transactions, and can delay issue resolution when vendor services are degraded or unavailable. The proposed platform adopts a redundancy architecture in which key transactional tables are replicated 1:1 into a Microsoft SQL Server environment, preserving raw data fidelity and traceability, while an ELT workflow refreshes replicated data within defined boundaries to support safe reruns and late adjustments. Report calculations are reconstructed at a semantic layer exposed through a FastAPI service, enabling schema-on-read transformations that keep business rules versioned and auditable and decouple the reporting portal from database implementation details. The project adopts an iterative and incremental development approach in which report requirements are inferred from vendor exports and stakeholder feedback, implemented through a Python-based ELT workflow using SQLAlchemy and pyodbc, and delivered through FastAPI and a React-based reporting portal. The project targets fifteen high-priority sales and payment reports and evaluates report-level parity through iterative reverse engineering and reconciliation-driven validation against vendor exports for matched parameters (e.g., outlet and date range), complemented by cross-report consistency checks to support fitness-for-use in financial reporting. The expected outcome is an independently operated reporting environment that improves accessibility for Finance and Operations, supports continuity of reporting during vendor disruptions, and provides a defensible basis for ongoing report maintenance through documented validation evidence.
